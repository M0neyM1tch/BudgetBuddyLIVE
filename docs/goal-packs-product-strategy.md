# Goal Packs Product Strategy

Prepared: 2026-06-30

## Product Decision

BudgetBuddy V2 should remain a universal personal finance app. The pivot is not a narrow first-home product. The pivot is a Goal Packs layer that makes the existing app personalize around the user's top financial priority.

Primary wedge:

> Turn your financial goals into a plan you can actually follow.

## Core Promise

The authenticated dashboard should answer five questions quickly:

- What is my top financial priority?
- How far along am I?
- Am I on track?
- What changed since last time?
- What is the next small action?

## MVP Goal Packs

Start with four packs:

- Emergency Fund
- Debt Payoff
- Major Purchase / Home Fund
- Custom Goal

Home Fund is intentionally handled through the Major Purchase engine first. Canada-specific FHSA, TFSA, tax refund, and mortgage-readiness logic is deferred until the core loop proves useful.

## Deferred Goal Packs

- Home Fund as a country-aware standalone pack
- Retirement
- General Savings
- Bill Reliability
- Wealth / Net Worth

## Product Rules

- Keep the current V2 layout and navigation.
- The dashboard changes based on the active priority.
- Transactions remain the cash-flow proof engine.
- Goals and Debts remain source-of-truth records.
- Analytics explains what changed the plan.
- Calculator becomes dynamic simulators over time.
- Goal Packs are scenarios and estimates, not advice.

## Copy Rules

Use:

- estimated
- projected
- based on the information you entered
- scenario
- common approach
- consider

Avoid:

- guaranteed
- approved
- best investment
- tax optimization
- you should invest
- financial advice
- mortgage-ready without qualification

## Free Release Principle

Goal Packs should ship as a useful free product before premium gating, Plaid, billing, or upgrade overlays. Monetization belongs after users feel value from the new active-priority dashboard and next-action loop.
